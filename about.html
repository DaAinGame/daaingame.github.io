About

Hi, I'm Timothy
I photograph things
that move me.

It started in fifth or sixth grade, in a photography unit that most kids probably forgot about by the next week. I didn't. I remember walking around our small Kansas town with a camera, looking for something worth keeping. I found it in a lonely, decrepit barn sitting in a hayfield, shot through a barbed wire fence in black and white. My teacher liked it enough to offer to buy it. That was the moment.

I took every photography class I could after that through high school and into college. The gear changed, the subjects changed, but the feeling never did. That quiet satisfaction of finding a frame in something ordinary. Of telling a story.

Astrophotography came more recently. I've always had a thing for space. Hubble images, NASA missions, the James Webb. I'm particularly in love with the Pillars of Creation, my ultimate goal to capture them someday. One night I went out to shoot the Milky Way untracked, just to see what would happen. What happened was I got completely hooked. I bought a tracker the next week. The first images were, to put it nicely, rough. Noisy, soft, humbling. But over the past eight months I've refined my skills and my process, and now I can't imagine not going out on a clear night.

There's something I love about standing under the sky and being a little more familiar with it each time. Knowing which constellation that is. Getting polar aligned, framing up, and then seeing that faint glow appear on the LCD. A galaxy or nebula that's been out there for millions of years, and I just caught a little of its light. That never gets old.

This site is a living archive of all of it. New work goes up as it gets made.

Timothy Maclaskey  ·  Kansas
